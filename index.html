ALERTS IN EFFECT

Park Closures

  • Sequoias Are Only Accessible Via Kings Canyon National Park

    If you wish to see giant sequoia trees, please enter the Kings Canyon Entrance along CA Highway 180. You cannot reach sequoia trees from the entrance to Sequoia Park along Highway 198. The Generals Highway between Kings Canyon and Sequoia is closed.

  • Facilities, Services Closed Due to KNP Complex Wildfire

    Many roads, lodges, and services are closed in both parks. Expect limited access and bring everything you need for your visit.

Caution

  • Protect Wildlife-Drive Slow and Watch for Wildlife on Roads

    Due to the recent closure of the Generals Highway from the wildfire, park animals have not had to share the road with visitors for some time. Wildlife have been active on roads. Always follow the speed limit and watch for animals traveling on the road.

Basic Information

  • Physical Address >

    47050 Generals Highway
    Three Rivers, CA 93271

  • Directions >

    Two highways enter the parks. Hwy 180 from Fresno leads east to Kings Canyon National Park, then continues 30 miles east to Cedar Grove. Hwy 198 from Visalia leads east to Sequoia National Park via Three Rivers. Inside the parks, Highway 198 becomes the Generals Highway, which connects 198 to 180. Vehicles over 22-feet long should enter the parks via Highway 180. In winter, the Generals Highway between the parks often closes. Chains may be required on park roads. No roads cross these parks east to west.

  • Weather >

    Because of the extreme elevation range in these parks, weather conditions vary widely between areas. In summer, the sequoia groves have comfortable temperatures and the foothills are hot and dry. Snow lingers on high-mountain passes well into summer. In fall, some areas close or reduce their hours. Winter brings snow to sequoia groves and rain to the foothills. Be prepared for tire chain requirements. Spring is a great time to hike in the foothills and snow begins to melt at higher elevations.

  • Fees & Passes >

    Vehicle Pass - $35.00
    This pass is valid for 1-7 days and includes everyone traveling in a single vehicle for Sequoia & Kings Canyon National Parks and Hume Lake District of Sequoia National Forest/Giant Sequoia National Monument.

  • Visitor Centers >

    This visitor center is one mile past the Ash Mountain entrance station along the Generals Highway. Stop here for information, maps, books, gifts, and restrooms. Browse exhibits about the ecology and human history of the foothills, and join a free ranger-led program.

  • Internet & Cellular Access >

    Free public WiFi is offered by the park at two visitor centers: Kings Canyon Visitor Center in Grant Grove Village, and Foothills Visitor Center near the Sequoia park entrance. No password is needed. WiFi is also available for guests of park lodges. Cell service varies in the park but is very limited. AT&T service is available near the Sequoia Park entrance. Verizon service is available in the Grant Grove area. Service is occasionally available in other areas, depending on conditions.
